    § 01 · The Foundation

    A Europe
    that holds.

    ESRF.net is growing organically and step by step into a European, cross-sector resilience platform: carefully built, reliably maintained and carried by organisations that contribute to Europe's resilience.

    Security is not just a mandate.
    It is a responsibility
    we carry, together.
    — ESRF.net Charter, Art. 01
    What is ESRF.net

    A framework
    built from below.

    Decentralised by design, civic in purpose, European in reach.

    ESRF.net is a curated directory of 2,083 organisations across 30 countries whose work, visible or otherwise, strengthens the continent's capacity to innovate, cooperate and build lasting resilience through collective effort.

    Where governments set policy and international bodies coordinate, ESRF.net unites the entrepreneurs, institutions and first responders whose daily practice is the actual substrate of resilience. We classify organisations across ten vital sectors — aligned with the EU NIS2 Directive.

    We are not a regulator. We are a community of practice.

    Stichting ESRF European Security and Resilience Fund receives donations and contributions to strengthen the network and expand its reach. Listings are free; contributions are welcome; participation is a matter of stewardship, not subscription.

    § 02 · The Moral Compass

    Five principles.
    One community.

    Nº 01 · Prevent

    Vigilance

    To see clearly before others react. The first pillar is the patient, structural art of foresight — reading signals, mapping scenarios and building understanding that stays one step ahead. Vigilance is not anxiety; it is the quiet confidence of a community that pays attention together.

    Nº 02 · Protect

    Stewardship

    To strengthen what sustains us. The second pillar is the discipline of reinforcing physical, digital and institutional foundations. Stewardship means one does not own what one protects; one holds it in trust for everyone who depends on it — and makes it stronger for those who come next.

    Nº 03 · Prepare

    Empowerment

    To stand ready, together. The third pillar is the cultivation of optionality — the capacity to act with confidence when a plan meets weather. Preparedness is not stockpiling; it is the shared competence to adapt.

    Nº 04 · Respond

    Solidarity

    To act as one when it matters. The fourth pillar is the virtue of arriving — reliably, quickly, together. Solidarity is not a sentiment; it is a practice that must be rehearsed long before it is required.

    Nº 05 · Recover

    Renewal

    To rise stronger than before. The fifth pillar is the discipline of absorbing shocks and restarting systems with innovation at the core. Renewal treats every recovery as an opportunity to improve what the previous version could not — turning experience into engineering, and setbacks into breakthroughs.

    § 03 · How it works

    Transparent
    by design.

    Governance, methodology, selection — all in the open.

    Legal entity

    ESRF.net is operated by Stichting ESRF European Security and Resilience Fund, a not-for-profit foundation registered in the Netherlands (KvK). The foundation — referred to as ESRF.net when acting as community enabler — exists solely to maintain the framework, fund the editorial operation, and protect the independence of the directory.

    Methodology

    Organisations are classified across ten vital sectors aligned with the EU NIS2 Directive and the Critical Entities Resilience (CER) Directive. Each listing is editorially reviewed against three criteria: (1) the organisation's work must demonstrably contribute to European security or resilience, (2) it must operate in or serve at least one of the 29 covered countries, and (3) it must have a verifiable public presence (website, chamber of commerce registration, or equivalent).

    Selection criteria

    ESRF.net does not sell listings, rank its members, or charge subscription fees. Organisations may request a listing or be identified through open-source intelligence. Every listing is reviewed by the editorial team against the sector taxonomy and the three criteria above. Listings that no longer meet the criteria are archived, not deleted — transparency applies in both directions.

    Stichting vs. network

    The foundation (Stichting ESRF European Security and Resilience Fund) is the legal entity. The network is the community of 2,083 organisations. The foundation does not own, control or represent the organisations it lists — it curates the directory, maintains the atlas, publishes the dispatch, and channels contributions into initiatives that make the network grow and more robust. The organisations themselves are sovereign; the directory is a public good.

    § 04 · How to join

    Take your
    place.

    A listing is free. Stewardship is the only dues we collect.

    Any organisation — commercial, institutional, non-profit — whose work strengthens European security and resilience is welcome on the atlas. There are three steps:

    1. Request a listing. Fill in the request-a-listing form with your organisation’s name, a one-paragraph description and your primary sector of practice.
    2. Align with the charter. Every listed organisation subscribes to the ESRF.net charter — one page, no fine print.
    3. Appear on the atlas. Your listing goes live on the directory, the map and, where relevant, the dispatch.

    Contributions to the foundation — financial or in-kind — are welcome but never required. ESRF.net does not sell listings, rank its members, or charge subscription fees.

    § 05 · Contribute

    Strengthen the network.

    Stichting ESRF European Security and Resilience Fund receives donations and contributions from the European community and reinvests them into initiatives that make the network grow and more resilient.

    Every contribution — however modest — goes directly to maintaining the atlas, curating the directory, publishing the Dispatch, and expanding the reach of the network across Europe. No shareholder returns, no hidden overheads.
