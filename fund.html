    great resilience.

    ESRF.net is sustained by the European community it serves. Not by a handful of large donors, but by the collective power of many small contributions.     § 02 · The idea

    Many small
    donations, one strong foundation.

    Many small contributions, one resilient network.

    Stichting ESRF European Security and Resilience Fund is a not-for-profit foundation. It does not sell listings, charge membership fees, or accept pay-to-rank arrangements. The directory is — and will remain — a public good.

    To cover its operating costs, ESRF.net is funded through three channels: minimal, non-intrusive advertising (via Google AdSense), voluntary donations from the European community, and corporate sponsorships. Advertising revenue helps keep the lights on; donations and sponsorships fund the growth and editorial independence of the network.

    The foundation is designed around a principle: many small contributions are more powerful than a few large ones. When hundreds of organisations and individuals each give a modest amount, the network becomes self-sustaining, independent, and accountable to the community — not to any single benefactor. This distributed funding model mirrors the distributed resilience the framework itself promotes. It is not just a funding strategy; it is the philosophy made practical.

    One donation is a gesture. A thousand donations are an unstoppable force.
    European contributions only. To preserve the independence and European character of the framework, the foundation accepts donations exclusively from organisations and individuals based in Europe, and from members of the ESRF.net network.

    Every euro contributed goes to maintaining the atlas, curating the directory, publishing the Dispatch, and expanding the reach of the network across 30 European countries. There are no hidden overheads — transparency is the only policy.

    § 03 · Where it goes

    Transparent by principle.

    Every contribution to the foundation is allocated across four pillars of operation. No funds go to shareholder returns — there are none.

    40 %

    Directory & Atlas

    Editorial curation, verification of listings, data quality, and the interactive map infrastructure.

    25 %

    Technology & Hosting

    Servers, multilingual infrastructure across 27 languages, security, and continuous platform development.

    20 %

    Dispatch & Outreach

    Publishing the security and resilience bulletin, country analytics, and network communications.

    15 %

    Governance & Legal

    Foundation administration, compliance, financial reporting, and the annual transparency report.

    § 04 · Ways to contribute

    Every form
    of support counts.

    Financial, practical, or simply spreading the word.

    One-time donation

    A single contribution of any amount. Whether it is €5 or €500 — every donation is recorded transparently and channelled directly into the network's operations. No amount is too small.

    Recurring monthly gift

    A modest recurring contribution — even €2 per month — provides the foundation with predictable income. Predictability allows planning: more languages, more countries, better tools. Recurring donors are acknowledged in the annual transparency report.

    Corporate sponsorship

    Organisations seeking a deeper partnership can explore our sponsorship tiers — Founding Patron, Programme Partner, or Community Supporter. Sponsorships are disclosed transparently and never influence editorial decisions.

    In-kind contributions

    Not every contribution is monetary. Translation help, data verification, editorial input, or technical expertise — the community's skills are the network's greatest asset. Contact us if you would like to contribute your time or expertise.

    Spread the word

    Share ESRF.net with colleagues, partners, and networks. Every new listing, every new reader, every shared article strengthens the community. Visibility is a form of contribution.

    § 06 · Transparency

    Open books,
    open doors.

    Every euro accounted for, every decision disclosed.

    Stichting ESRF European Security and Resilience Fund publishes an annual transparency report detailing all income, expenditures, and allocation decisions. The foundation is registered in the Netherlands and subject to Dutch non-profit governance law.

    No individual donor — however generous — is granted editorial influence. The separation between funding and editorial is absolute: contributors fund the infrastructure, the editorial team guards the independence. This is not a policy; it is the reason the network exists.

    • Annual financial report published on this website
    • All sponsorships and major donations disclosed by name and tier
    • Foundation registered as Stichting under Dutch law (KvK)
    • No shareholder returns — 100 % of income goes to network operations
    • Minimal, non-intrusive advertising (Google AdSense) to sustain baseline operations
    • Donations accepted exclusively from European sources and ESRF.net network members
