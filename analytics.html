    § 05 · Analytics

    Patterns in
    the network.

    Three figures published with every issue. Where the community is concentrated, where new connections are forming, and how the ten sectors distribute across countries.

    Fig. 01

    Organisations per sector

    Emergency responders and continuity professionals form the established core of the community; energy and transport represent the newest frontier of growth.

    Fig. 01 — Counts of organisations in each of the ten vital sectors. Bar colour follows the sector itself.

    Fig. 02

    Organisations per country

    Thirty nations, each contributing a share of the whole. The Netherlands, United Kingdom and Germany currently carry the most; Malta and Bulgaria the fewest — a balance the framework is actively redistributing.

    Fig. 02 — Distribution of listed organisations across the 30 countries represented on the atlas.

    Fig. 03

    Sectors across countries

    How the ten vital sectors distribute across the fifteen most represented nations. Each bar is a country; each colour, a sector. Where one colour dominates, the local ecosystem is specialised; where the spread is even, it is diversified.

    Fig. 03 — Sector composition of the top fifteen countries by number of listed organisations. Bar colour follows the sector palette.
